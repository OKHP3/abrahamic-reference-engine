## Non-denominational / Independent Evangelical

Non-denominational churches are the fastest-growing segment of American Christianity. They typically share evangelical theology without denominational affiliation or oversight.

**US population context:** included within the ~25% Evangelical Protestant figure (Pew 2023). No separate Pew category.

**Key characteristics:**
- No formal creed beyond Scripture; some use informal statements of faith
- Mega-church model common; services often contemporary in style
- Strong personal relationship with Jesus as a core framing
- Independent governance; no bishop or council above the local congregation
- Wide theological diversity despite shared evangelical identity

**Translation preference:** NIV (most common in contemporary evangelical contexts), ESV, KJV. In the free build: `web`, `kjv`, `asv`.

**Note:** ARE does not have a separate denomination entry for non-denominational -- this segment is captured within the Evangelical Protestant category.

## Pentecostal / Charismatic Christianity

Pentecostalism originated in the 1906 Azusa Street Revival in Los Angeles. The Charismatic movement (beginning ~1960) brought similar emphases into mainline and Catholic churches.

**US population context:** included within the Evangelical Protestant figure (Pew 2023). No separate Pew category at 1%+ threshold.

**Key characteristics:**
- Emphasis on "gifts of the Spirit" (charismata): speaking in tongues, prophecy, healing
- Baptism of the Holy Spirit as a distinct post-conversion experience
- Expressive worship; spontaneous prayer; expectation of miraculous signs
- Global Pentecostalism is the fastest-growing Christian movement worldwide

**Key passages:** Acts 2:1-4 (Pentecost); 1 Corinthians 12-14 (spiritual gifts); Mark 16:17-18 (signs of belief)

**Translation preference:** NIV, KJV. In the free build: `kjv`, `web`, `bbe`.

## Reformed / Calvinist Christianity

A theological tradition within both Evangelical and Mainline Protestantism, emphasizing God's sovereignty in salvation.

**US population context:** distributed across Evangelical and Mainline categories; no separate Pew category.

**Key distinctives:**
- Five Points of Calvinism (TULIP): Total depravity, Unconditional election, Limited atonement, Irresistible grace, Perseverance of the saints
- Westminster Confession of Faith (Presbyterian) or Belgic Confession (Dutch Reformed)
- Strong tradition of formal theological education

**Key denominations:** Presbyterian Church in America (PCA), Reformed Church in America (RCA), Orthodox Presbyterian Church (OPC)

## Messianic Judaism

Jewish believers who accept Jesus (Yeshua) as the Messiah while maintaining Jewish identity and practice.

**Scope note:** Messianic Judaism is a hybrid category -- Jewish ethnic/cultural identity combined with Christian theological affirmation. ARE treats it as context-only and does not display it as a separate denomination. It does not meet the 1% US threshold as a distinct tradition.

**ARE behavior:** not displayed as a denomination; not included in cross-tradition comparisons as a separate category.

---

## Anglican / Episcopal

The Episcopal Church (US) is the American branch of the Anglican Communion -- the worldwide family of churches in communion with the Archbishop of Canterbury.

**US population context:** included within Mainline Protestant (Pew 2023).

**Key distinctives:**
- Via media ("middle way") between Catholic and Reformed traditions
- Book of Common Prayer as the liturgical foundation; multiple editions (1662, 1979, 2019)
- Apostolic succession maintained through historic episcopate
- Full inclusion of women and LGBTQ+ clergy in the Episcopal Church (TEC)

**Translation preference:** NRSV (formal services), KJV (traditional). In the free build: `kjv`, `web`.
