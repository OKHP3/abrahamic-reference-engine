# OverKill Hill P³ Abrahamic Reference Engine – UI Copy

Welcome to the **Abrahamic Reference Engine**, a neutral and scholarly assistant designed to help you explore sacred texts across Judaism, Christianity, Islam, and, optionally, Hinduism and Buddhism. This tool retrieves verified passages directly from public APIs, cites translations and versions clearly, and offers cautious context. It is not a preacher or a theologian—it is a librarian guiding you to the text.

## Getting Started

- **Set your preferences:** You can specify your default tradition (e.g., Christianity or Islam) and choose preferred translations (e.g., ESV for the Bible or Sahih International for the Qur’an). Use a command like `Set my default tradition to Judaism and default Bible translation to NRSV`.
- **Retrieve a passage:** Ask for a verse by book and reference, such as `Fetch Genesis 1:1` or `Give me Surah 2 Ayah 255 in two translations.`
- **Compare themes:** To see how different traditions address a topic, try `Compare passages about justice in the Bible, Qur’an, and Tanakh.`
- **Adjust context level:** Control how much commentary you receive with `Set commentary level to none`, `brief`, or `scholarly`.

## What to Expect

- The assistant will provide **verbatim quotes** with metadata indicating the translation and source.
- Any **contextual notes** will be clearly separated from the quoted text and will avoid doctrinal claims.
- If a requested text is not available or outside the current scope, the assistant will tell you and, where appropriate, suggest alternatives.

## Error Messages

- **Unknown reference:** *“I’m sorry, I could not find that passage. Please check the book, chapter, and verse.”*
- **Unsupported request:** *“This tradition or text is not currently available in this version of the engine.”*
- **Ambiguous paraphrase:** *“I couldn’t locate an exact match for that phrase. Here are possible related passages…”*
- **License limitations:** *“This translation requires attribution or is not accessible via our providers. Please choose another translation.”*

## Safety and Neutrality

The engine follows strict guidelines to remain neutral across traditions. It will not rank beliefs or offer proselytizing commentary. For contested passages or sensitive topics, it will present multiple mainstream interpretations without taking a position. If you ask for hate speech or to denigrate a belief, it will decline the request.

We hope this tool enhances your study, facilitates interfaith understanding, and provides a reliable reference point for conversations about scripture.
