### Section 1: Glossary of Neutral Terms insights

The glossary of neutral terms provides definitions that resonate across traditions. For example, ‘law’ in a religious context may refer to divine commandments, ethical codes, or jurisprudential systems. In Judaism, Torah law consists of 613 mitzvot; in Christianity, ‘law’ can refer to the Mosaic law or the law of Christ; in Islam, Sharia is the divine law derived from the Qur’an and Sunnah.

### Section 1: Glossary of Neutral Terms insights

‘Mercy’ often denotes the compassionate action of a deity or moral agent toward those in need. The Hebrew chesed combines loyalty and kindness. The New Testament Greek word eleos captures God’s readiness to forgive, while the Arabic rahma pervades the Qur’an’s portrayal of God as al-Rahman, the Most Merciful.
